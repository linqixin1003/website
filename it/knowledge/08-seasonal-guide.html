Spring Birding (March-May)
Spring is often considered the best season for birdingüå±. Migrants return from their wintering grounds, residents establish territories, and bird activity reaches its annual peak. Early morning hours are especially productive as birds sing to establish territories and attract mates.
Spring Highlights by Month:
  • March: Early migrants arrive, waterfowl return, singing increases
  • April: Peak warbler migration, territory establishment, nest building begins
  • May: Late migrants arrive, courtship displays, early nesting activity
üåÖ Spring Birding Tips
Visit diverse habitats, focus on early morning hours, listen for increased singing, watch for courtship behaviors, and check migration hotspots regularly.

Early summer offers opportunities to observe nesting behavior, while late summer brings family groups and post-breeding dispersalüè†. Young birds learning to forage and fly provide entertaining and educational viewing.
Fall Birding (September-November)
Fall migration can be even more spectacular than spring, though it's often more spread out over timeü¶Ö. Hawk migration is particularly impressive during fall, with raptors concentrating along ridges and coastlines as they head south.
Fall Migration Timing:
  • September: Shorebirds, early warblers, and hawk migration begins
  • October: Peak hawk migration, sparrows, and late warblers
  • November: Waterfowl migration, winter residents arrive
