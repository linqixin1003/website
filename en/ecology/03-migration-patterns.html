üó∫Ô∏è Major Migration Routes

Global bird migration follows several main flight paths, known as "migration flyways":

üåè East Asian-Australasian Flyway

Connecting Alaska, Siberia with Southeast Asia, Australia

  • Main species: Plovers and sandpipers, geese and ducks, raptors
  • Key stopover sites: Yellow Sea wetlands, Yangtze River estuary
  • Threats: Wetland loss, sea level rise

üåç Central Asian Flyway

Connecting Siberia with South Asia, Africa

  • Main species: Cranes, raptors, songbirds
  • Key stopover sites: Caspian Sea, Aral Sea region
  • Threats: Habitat degradation, climate change

üåé Americas Flyway

Connecting North America with Central and South America

  • Main species: Warblers, flycatchers, hummingbirds
  • Key stopover sites: Gulf of Mexico coast
  • Threats: Deforestation, urbanization

üåç European-African Flyway

Connecting Europe with sub-Saharan Africa

  • Main species: Swallows, flycatchers, storks
  • Key stopover sites: Mediterranean coast
  • Threats: Desertification, human disturbance
