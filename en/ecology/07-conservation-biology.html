🚨 Threats Facing Birds
Global bird populations face unprecedented survival threats; understanding these threats is the foundation for developing effective conservation strategies.

🏗️ Habitat Loss and Fragmentation

  • Deforestation and land development
  • Wetland filling and drying
  • Grassland conversion to farmland
  • Coastal development and land reclamation
  • Habitat quality degradation

🌡️ Climate Change

  • Changes in temperature and precipitation patterns
  • Increased extreme weather events
  • Rising sea levels
  • Phenological mismatches
  • Distribution range shifts

🏭 Environmental Pollution

  • Pesticide and chemical pollution
  • Plastic pollution
  • Heavy metal contamination
  • Noise and light pollution
  • Oil spills

🎯 Overexploitation

  • Illegal hunting and trade
  • Egg collection
  • Feather trade
  • Pet trade
  • Traditional medicine use

👾 Invasive Species

  • Competition for native food resources
  • Predation of native birds
  • Disease transmission
  • Alteration of habitat structure
  • Hybridization threats
📊 Conservation Status Assessment
Scientific assessment of bird conservation status is the foundation for establishing conservation priorities.

🔴 IUCN Red List Categories

  • Extinct (EX): Species completely disappeared
  • Extinct in the Wild (EW): Only captive individuals remain
  • Critically Endangered (CR): Facing extremely high extinction risk
  • Endangered (EN): Facing very high extinction risk
  • Vulnerable (VU): Facing high extinction risk
  • Near Threatened (NT): Close to qualifying for a threatened status
  • Least Concern (LC): Not facing extinction threat

📏 Assessment Criteria

  • Population size change trends
  • Distribution range size and changes
  • Habitat quality and area
  • Severity of threat factors
  • Population structure and breeding success rate
