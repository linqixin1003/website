Key Processes in the Rock Cycle
üî• Melting and Crystallization

When rocks are subjected to extreme heat (700-1200¬∞C), they melt to form magma. As this magma cools, minerals crystallize to create igneous rocks. The cooling rate determines crystal size‚Äîslow cooling produces large crystals, while rapid cooling creates fine-grained textures.

üåä Weathering and Erosion

Physical and chemical processes break down rocks into smaller particles. Water, wind, ice, and biological activity transport these sediments to new locations where they accumulate in layers, eventually forming sedimentary rocks through lithification.

‚ö° Metamorphism

Existing rocks undergo transformation due to heat, pressure, or chemical fluids without completely melting. This process can occur through contact with hot magma, regional tectonic forces, or hydrothermal activity, creating entirely new mineral assemblages.
