Types of Metamorphism
üî• Contact Metamorphism

Occurs when rocks are heated by nearby magma intrusions. Heat causes localized changes in surrounding rocks, creating metamorphic aureoles. This type of metamorphism typically affects smaller areas but can achieve high metamorphic grades.

Typical Products: Hornfels, Marble, Skarn
üèîÔ∏è Regional Metamorphism

Results from large-scale geological processes such as mountain building, where vast areas of rock are subjected to increased temperature and pressure over long periods. This is the most common type of metamorphism, forming extensive metamorphic rock belts.

Typical Products: Schist, Gneiss, Slate
‚ö° Dynamic Metamorphism

Occurs along fault zones where rocks are subjected to intense pressure and shearing forces, creating mylonites and other deformed rock types. This metamorphism is primarily driven by mechanical stress.

Typical Products: Mylonite, Fault Breccia

üîç Identification Tips

The key to identifying metamorphic rocks lies in observing mineral arrangement, degree of crystallization, and special structures. Foliation, schistosity, and banded structures are all important indicators of metamorphism. Use a hand lens to observe the oriented arrangement of minerals and recrystallization features.

Geological Significance and Practical Applications
Metamorphic rocks have important significance in geological research, as they record Earth's internal temperature-pressure conditions and tectonic history üåç. By studying metamorphic rocks, scientists can reconstruct the evolutionary processes of ancient orogenic belts and understand the history of plate tectonic movements.
In practical applications, many metamorphic rocks have important economic value üí∞. Marble is widely used in architectural decoration and sculptural art, slate is used as roofing material and flooring, gneiss is used for building stone, and certain metamorphic rocks contain precious metals and gemstone minerals.
The study of metamorphic rocks also helps understand deep Earth processes and lithospheric evolution üî¨. They provide direct evidence of Earth's internal conditions, helping scientists build more accurate geodynamic models.
